## How to Think About ROI for a Churn Prevention Tool

The ROI of a customer health intelligence platform has two components:

1. **Retained ARR** — revenue that would have churned without early intervention
2. **CS team efficiency** — time saved per CSM per week that can be redirected toward expansion or higher-value activity

A simple way to frame the ROI math:

> If Sentio helps you retain 2 additional accounts per quarter that would have churned, and your average ACV is $30,000, that's $60,000 in retained ARR per quarter — 1.7× the cost of a Growth tier contract in a single quarter.

The efficiency gains are real but harder to quantify in dollar terms. The retention math is straightforward.

## The NRR Math

Every CS leader knows that NRR improvement compounds. A simple illustration:

| ARR | NRR | ARR at End of Year |
|---|---|---|
| $20M | 95% | $19M |
| $20M | 100% | $20M |
| $20M | 105% | $21M |
| $20M | 110% | $22M |

Moving NRR from 95% to 100% at $20M ARR is worth $1M in retained revenue year-over-year. Moving it from 100% to 105% adds another $1M. The numbers scale linearly with ARR.

Sentio's job is to move NRR in the right direction by catching the accounts at risk before the renewal conversation.

## What Drives the Best Results

Based on patterns across Sentio's customer base (illustrative), the teams that see the strongest outcomes share three practices:

1. **They act on the playbook within 48 hours of it firing.** Accounts flagged at 60–90 days out have a much higher recovery rate than accounts actioned at 10–30 days out. Speed of response matters.

2. **They use Sentio's task queue as the daily starting point.** Teams that open Sentio first thing in the morning — before email, before Slack — and work the prioritized task queue see better outcomes than teams that check Sentio occasionally.

3. **They review the CSM variance report quarterly.** The best playbook practices are usually already happening inside the team. Making them visible and standard is where the consistency gains come from.
