**Agentic Marketing Solution** 

*Take-Home Assessment · Docket AI · Customer Success Program Manager* 

**The Problem** 

A B2B SaaS marketing team is losing pipeline at every stage of the funnel. 

Inbound demo requests sit in a queue — nobody knows which ones to call first. The website chatbot greets  visitors but doesn't qualify them, doesn't book meetings, and never escalates to a human when a real prospect  shows up. SDRs are blasting the same template to every outbound lead, and conversion is dropping. Past leads  — months of demo requests that never converted — sit in a graveyard, possibly re-engageable, untouched. 

The CMO wants this fixed. She doesn't care which part you tackle, or how. She wants pipeline. 

**The Ask** 

 Build an agent or tool that addresses one or more of these gaps. 

 Use Claude, Codex, GPT, GitHub, or any tools you choose. 

 Be opinionated. Pick the angle you think creates the most leverage. 

**Deliverables** 

 A 5-minute Loom video walking through the workflow on your tool. 

 A document explaining the solution — what you built, how it works, and why you designed it that way.  The code, shared via GitHub or equivalent. 

**A Note on Assumed Facts** 

The brief above is intentionally minimal. You will need to assume context — the company, the product, the ICP, the buyer, success metrics, anything else — in order to design and build a meaningful solution. That is expected and welcome. State your assumptions clearly in your document. 

When we say "no hallucinated facts" in the disqualifiers below, we are not talking about the context you invent to anchor your demo. We are talking about your agent generating false information at runtime — fabricated  stats, made-up customer references, invented pricing, fictional company details in outreach emails, etc. 

**In short:** 

 Inventing a fictional company with a defined ICP to demo against: fine, encouraged.  Your agent outputting a fake funding round or fabricated tech-stack claim about a real lead's company: not  fine.
