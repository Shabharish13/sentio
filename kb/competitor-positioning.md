# Sentio — Competitive Positioning

> This document describes how Sentio positions relative to alternatives. All competitive claims are based on Sentio's own positioning and publicly available information — not fabricated weaknesses. Sentio never disparages competitors; it describes segment fit.

---

## The Competitive Landscape

Sentio competes primarily with:
1. **Gainsight** — enterprise CS platform
2. **Totango** — mid-market CS platform
3. **ChurnZero** — SMB / lower mid-market CS platform
4. **Spreadsheets and manual processes** — the most common "competitor" by volume

The competitive question is almost always one of segment fit and implementation cost — not feature-by-feature comparison.

---

## Gainsight

### What Gainsight Is
Gainsight is the category-defining enterprise customer success platform. It has comprehensive features across health scoring, playbooks, revenue intelligence, and CS team management. It's the market leader by revenue and enterprise market share.

### Who It's Built For
Gainsight is built for enterprise CS organizations: companies with 20+ CSMs, a dedicated CS Ops function, and the resources to manage a sophisticated platform. Its implementation typically requires a Gainsight-certified administrator or a professional services engagement.

### How Sentio Positions Against Gainsight
**Sentio is not trying to replace Gainsight for enterprise customers.** For a company with 30 CSMs, a CS Ops team of 3, and an IT department that can manage integrations, Gainsight may be the right choice.

Sentio's position:
- **Implementation speed:** Sentio is live in under a day. Gainsight implementations for mid-market companies typically take 3–6 months, including a professional services engagement.
- **Total cost of ownership:** Gainsight's published pricing starts well above Sentio's Growth tier. Implementation and admin overhead add further cost.
- **Fit for team size:** For companies with 5–15 CSMs, Gainsight's feature depth often exceeds what the team can actually use, and the maintenance burden falls on people who have other jobs.

**The right question to ask:** How much of Gainsight are you actually using? Most mid-market Gainsight customers use the health scorecard and playbooks — both of which Sentio covers, without the implementation and maintenance overhead.

## Totango

### What Totango Is
Totango is a customer success platform targeting mid-market SaaS companies. It offers health scoring, lifecycle management, and playbooks with a segment-based organizational model.

### How Sentio Positions Against Totango
Totango's health scoring is largely configured by the customer: you define the metrics, the weights, and the thresholds. This gives you flexibility but requires CS Ops expertise to set up correctly and maintain.

Sentio's position:
- **Automated signal aggregation vs. manual configuration:** Sentio's scoring model learns from account behavior patterns rather than requiring you to define the rules. For teams without a dedicated CS Ops function, this removes the configuration burden.
- **Predictive vs. descriptive:** Totango surfaces current health state. Sentio predicts future churn risk at a 60–90 day horizon, which creates the intervention opportunity before it's visible in lagging metrics.

This is a closer competitive comparison than Gainsight — some prospects at the 200–400 employee range evaluate both. Sentio's differentiator in this comparison is the AI-driven prediction model and the speed of setup.

## ChurnZero

### What ChurnZero Is
ChurnZero is a customer success platform well-suited for SMB and lower mid-market CS teams. It has solid playbook automation and a user-friendly interface. It's particularly strong for companies with simpler, more homogeneous account profiles.

### How Sentio Positions Against ChurnZero
ChurnZero uses rule-based health scoring: you define thresholds (e.g., "no login in 30 days = at-risk"), and the system flags accounts that cross them. This works well when accounts are relatively simple and consistent.

Sentio's position:
- **Rule-based vs. predictive scoring:** For mid-market accounts with multi-stakeholder relationships, complex product footprints, and higher ACVs, rule-based scoring generates noise (false positives) and misses signals that don't fit predefined rules. Sentio's model identifies patterns in how accounts behave before churning — patterns that are hard to express as explicit rules.
- **Segment fit:** ChurnZero is well-designed for SMB accounts where ACV is lower and the account profile is simpler. Sentio is optimized for the 100–800 employee B2B SaaS company where account complexity justifies AI-driven scoring.

**The practical test:** If your team is already on ChurnZero and it's working, Sentio probably isn't worth evaluating. If your team feels like they're manually overriding ChurnZero's rules more than they're following them, that's a signal that rule-based scoring may not fit your account complexity.

## Spreadsheets and Manual Processes

### The "Before State"
The most common competitive situation Sentio encounters isn't another platform — it's a shared Google Sheet, a weekly standup where CSMs report on accounts they're worried about, and a renewal forecast built on gut feel.

This isn't a knock on teams that work this way. It's how most CS teams operate until the scale of the account book exceeds what can be managed manually — usually around 40–60 accounts per CSM.

### Sentio's Position vs. Spreadsheets
- **Completeness:** A CSM can only manually monitor the accounts they're already thinking about. Sentio monitors every account, all the time, against every connected signal.
- **Objectivity:** Manual health assessments are influenced by the CSM's relationship with the contact, their read of the last call, and their personal risk tolerance. Sentio's scoring is consistent and not relationship-dependent.
- **Proactivity:** The spreadsheet tells you what you put in it. Sentio tells you what you didn't know to look for.

**The honest conversation:** If you're managing the current account base on a spreadsheet and not missing renewals, the spreadsheet is working. Sentio becomes valuable when the account base grows faster than your team's ability to monitor it manually — or when a missed renewal that a spreadsheet should have caught is one too many.
