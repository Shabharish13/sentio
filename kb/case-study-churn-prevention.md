# Case Study: Churn Prevention at Scale

## How Meridian Analytics Retained $1.8M in At-Risk ARR Before Renewal Season

> **Fictional illustrative results.** Meridian Analytics is an invented company. All outcomes described are illustrative examples of the kind of results Sentio is designed to produce — not guaranteed results.

---

## Company Profile

- **Company:** Meridian Analytics *(fictional)*
- **Industry:** B2B SaaS — business intelligence and data analytics
- **Size:** ~210 employees
- **ARR at time of deployment:** ~$14M
- **CS team:** 8 CSMs, 1 VP of Customer Success
- **Sentio tier:** Growth

---

## The Problem

Meridian's VP of Customer Success, Priya, was 90 days out from a major renewal season — 40 accounts renewing in a 60-day window, representing $3.2M in ARR. Her team of 8 CSMs was already at capacity managing inbound support and quarterly business reviews.

The problem: Priya had no reliable way to know which of those 40 accounts were genuinely at risk and which were fine. Her team was using a shared spreadsheet updated manually — last refreshed three weeks prior. Two CSMs had recently flagged accounts they "had a bad feeling about," but there was no data behind it.

The standard approach was to schedule a health check call with every account 60 days before renewal. With 40 accounts and 8 CSMs, that meant each CSM needed to complete 5 renewal calls in addition to their normal workload — a logistical crunch that had caused two renewal misses the prior year.

---

## How Sentio Was Used

Meridian connected Sentio to HubSpot (CRM), Amplitude (product analytics), and Intercom (support) in about 6 hours on a Friday afternoon. By Monday morning, every account had a health score.

**What Sentio surfaced in the first 48 hours:**
- 12 accounts flagged in the At-Risk or Critical band
- 3 accounts Priya's team had assumed were healthy (no support tickets, no complaints) were in the 30–45 health score range due to a persistent drop in product engagement over the prior 8 weeks
- 1 account had a champion who had changed jobs — not caught by the CS team because the contact hadn't bounced yet
- 4 accounts had NPS detractors from a survey sent 6 weeks earlier — never actioned because the NPS data lived in a separate tool

Sentio prioritized the 12 at-risk accounts by churn probability and renewal proximity and generated a playbook for each.

---

## What Happened

Priya's team shifted focus from "call every account" to "call the right 12 accounts first, with a prepared playbook." Instead of generic health check calls, CSMs went into each conversation with specific context: what signal triggered the risk flag, what had changed, and what the recommended intervention was.

**Outcomes (illustrative):**

- Of the 12 flagged at-risk accounts, 9 were retained at renewal
- 3 accounts that were not flagged by Sentio (i.e., remained in the healthy band) also renewed without requiring a proactive call
- The 3 churned accounts had each triggered Critical-band flags more than 45 days before renewal — one had requested a contract modification (billing event), one had a detractor NPS score with a specific complaint that hadn't been actioned, and one had a champion who had left the company
- Total ARR retained among at-risk accounts: ~$1.8M of the $2.4M originally flagged as at-risk by Sentio
- CSM time saved on blanket renewal outreach: approximately 3–4 hours per CSM across the renewal window

**Priya's reflection (illustrative quote):**
> "We stopped doing wellness checks and started doing targeted interventions. The difference is that Sentio told us which 12 accounts mattered most, and gave us the reason why before we got on the call."

---

## Key Takeaway

The value wasn't just catching churn — it was catching it early enough to do something about it. Accounts flagged at 45 days out had time for a recovery playbook. Accounts flagged at 10 days out often didn't.

Sentio's 60–90 day prediction window is what creates the intervention opportunity. A health score that tells you an account is at risk the week before renewal is too late to be useful.
